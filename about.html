Hello! My name is Alex Jiang (he/him), and I am a second-year undergraduate student at the University of Pennsylvania. I'm currently pursuing a major in mechanical engineering and plan to pursue a second major in business with a management concentration.

I'm a sucker for practical problem solving, which is why I intend to pursue a career in the intersection between business and engineering. My specific academic interests include robotics, autonomous vehicles, product design, product management, and entrepreneurship. I'm still gaining more knowledge and exploring new fields of study, so these interests will likely change within the next years.

Since entering college, my priority has been self improvement. I've had successes and plenty of more failures, but, more importantly, I've leared many valuable lessons that shape my life philsophy. Here are some of my life principles:

  • Everyday is an opportunity to better yourself.
  • Don't stress about things that are out of your control. If you can fix it, fix it. If you can't, move on.
  • If you think about something you have to do that takes less than 5 minutes, do it immediately.
  • Prioritize creating meaningful friendships over "connecting" with a higher quantity of people
  • Ask to spend time 1 on 1 to really get to know someone.
  • If you have an opportunity to try something you've never done before, always* say yes.
  • School is overrated. Prioritize personal projects and independent learning over getting perfect grades.
  • Creativity is intrinsic to everyone. Find opportunities use it because school sucks it out of you.
  • The truth hurts, but it's necessary. Honesty is more important than protecting feelings.

This is all easier said than done of course, but I almost always try to abide by these principles. This website is mainly a portfolio, but I will be publishing a blog soon to share my two cents about whatever comes to mind.
